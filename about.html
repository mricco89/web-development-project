About Me

My name is Matthew Ricco. I am married and have 2 children. One is 1 year old, and the other is 5 years old. I work full-time as a Linux engineer using Yocto. I have a passion for anime, gardening, gaming, and playing drums. I attended Overbrook High School in Pine Hill, NJ, graduating in 2007. Initially pursuing a teaching degree at Camden County College, I shifted my career path towards game cabinet design, amusement ride repair. This put my college path on hold for some time. Around 2016, I relocated to Roswell, GA, where I continued my education remotely while working. I served as the QA manager for a game design company before transitioning into Linux and hardware engineering role within the same company. During this time, I also began pursuing a bachelor's degree program at Kennesaw State University.

My career journey has been marked by diverse experiences and a relentless pursuit of growth. At the age of 20, I achieved the role of District Manager for an arcade company, demonstrating early leadership and management skills. However, I soon realized that the earning potential did not align with the commitment required, prompting a transition to the IT department of a pharmaceutical company. Despite several years of dedicated service and even relocating with the company to Georgia, I found myself at a standstill in terms of professional growth after six years. Recognizing the need for a change, I made the bold decision to pursue further education in Linux administration and information systems while transitioning to a Quality Assurance role at a gaming company. Although this move initially meant a reduction in pay, the opportunity for personal and professional growth in a rapidly expanding company with a transformative journey from 30 to over 200 employees was undeniable. Balancing my roles as a full-time father and employee, I committed to steady progress by undertaking about five classes annually towards completing my bachelor's degree at KSU, acknowledging the limitations of my time and responsibilities.
